Question 1.3: What are the limitations of AI hyperkalemia detection?
Limitations and Considerations:

Technical Limitations:

  • False Positives (5-10%):
    • Other causes of peaked T waves: LVH, early repolarization, hyperacute MI
    • Baseline ECG variations in some patients
    • Need clinical context to interpret AI alert
  • False Negatives (10-15%):
    • Very rapid K+ rise may not show classic ECG changes
    • Concomitant calcium abnormalities mask hyperkalemia
    • Underlying bundle branch blocks obscure findings
  • Quantitative Accuracy:
    • AI predicts range (5.8-6.5), not exact value
    • 95% confidence intervals can be wide (¬±0.5-1.0 mEq/L)
    • Cannot replace laboratory measurement

Clinical Context Required:

  • Pre-test probability matters:
    • CKD Stage 4 patient ‚Üí high probability, high PPV
    • Young healthy patient ‚Üí low probability, lower PPV
    • Always confirm with laboratory K+ measurement
  • Not a replacement for clinical judgment:
    • AI is decision support tool, not autonomous diagnosis
    • Must integrate with symptoms, history, risk factors
    • Some patients need treatment regardless of exact K+ level
‚ö†Ô∏è Key Principle: AI-ECG hyperkalemia detection is a powerful screening tool that accelerates recognition, but laboratory confirmation remains the gold standard. Use AI alerts to guide urgency of workup and monitoring intensity, but always obtain definitive lab values before major treatment decisions (except life-threatening situations where empiric membrane stabilization may be appropriate).

Exercise 3: AFib Screening with AI

Question 3.1: How can AI detect AFib from sinus rhythm ECG?
AI Detection of "AFib Fingerprint" in Sinus Rhythm:

Mechanism - Structural/Electrical Remodeling:

  • Key Concept: Patients with paroxysmal AFib have underlying atrial remodeling even when in sinus rhythm
  • Remodeling Changes Detectable on SR ECG:
    • Atrial fibrosis ‚Üí altered conduction
    • LA enlargement ‚Üí P wave changes
    • Electrical instability ‚Üí micro-variabilities

Specific ECG Features AI Detects:

  1. P Wave Morphology Abnormalities:
    • Terminal negative portion of P wave in V1 (PTF-V1)
    • P wave duration >120 ms
    • Notched or bifid P waves
    • P wave amplitude variations
  2. P Wave Dispersion:
    • Variation in P wave duration across leads
    • Reflects inhomogeneous atrial conduction
    • Marker of atrial electrical instability
  3. PR Segment Variability:
    • Beat-to-beat PR interval changes (even subtle)
    • Suggests autonomic dysfunction
    • Predicts paroxysmal AFib
  4. Atrial Premature Complexes (APCs):
    • Frequent APCs predict AFib
    • AI detects even subtle APCs
    • APC burden correlates with AFib risk
  5. QRS-T Angle:
    • Reflects ventricular-atrial electrical relationship
    • Altered in patients with atrial disease
